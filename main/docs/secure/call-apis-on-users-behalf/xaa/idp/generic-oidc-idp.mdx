This page walks through configuring a generic OIDC identity provider for Cross App Access (XAA). You'll register the Requesting App and Resource App with your upstream IdP, configure a Cross App Access policy in the IdP, and configure an OpenID Connect Enterprise connection so Auth0 can federate with your IdP.

## Prerequisites

Before getting started, you need an OIDC-compliant enterprise IdP that supports issuing an [Identity Assertion Authorization Grant](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/draft-ietf-oauth-identity-assertion-authz-grant/) (ID-JAG). Confirm with your IdP vendor that Cross App Access is supported and enabled for your tenant.

## Register the Requesting App in your IdP

Register the Requesting App as an OIDC client in your upstream IdP. This is the client your users authenticate as when signing in to the Requesting App.

Note the Requesting App's `client_id` in your IdP; you'll need it later when configuring the Cross App Access policy.

## Register the Resource App in your IdP

Register the Resource App as an OIDC client in your upstream IdP. This client represents your Auth0 tenant to the IdP.

Note the Resource App's `client_id` and `client_secret` in your IdP; you'll need them when creating the Auth0 connection below.

## Configure the Cross App Access policy

In your IdP, configure the Cross App Access authorization policy to allow the Requesting App to connect to the Resource App. Refer to your IdP's documentation for how to define this policy — the specifics vary by vendor, but the policy must associate the Requesting App's client and the Resource App's client so that the IdP will issue an ID-JAG between them.

## Configure an OpenID Connect Enterprise connection in Auth0

In your Resource App's Auth0 tenant, [create an OpenID Connect Enterprise connection](/docs/authenticate/identity-providers/enterprise-identity-providers/configure-oidc-identity-provider) pointing to your IdP, using the `client_id` and `client_secret` of the Resource App you registered in your IdP.

To configure the connection for XAA:

1. Enable the **Cross App Access - Resource Application** role using the Auth0 Dashboard or Management API:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Auth0 Dashboard">
    Toggle on **Cross App Access - Resource Application**. This enables your Resource App to accept ID-JAGs issued by the enterprise IdP associated with this connection.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Management API">
    Make a `PATCH` request to the [`/connections/{YOUR_CONNECTION_ID}`](/docs/api/management/v2/connections/patch-connections-by-id) endpoint and set `cross_app_access_resource_app` to `active: true`:

    ```bash
    curl -L -X PATCH 'https://<YOUR_DOMAIN>/api/v2/connections/<YOUR_CONNECTION_ID>' \
      -H 'Content-Type: application/json' \
      -H 'Authorization: Bearer <YOUR_MANAGEMENT_API_TOKEN>' \
      -d '{"cross_app_access_resource_app": {"active": true}}'
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

2. Check that the **Callback URL** provided by Auth0 in the connection's settings matches the **Redirect URI** you configured for the Resource App in your upstream IdP.

Once the connection is created, you are ready to test the end-to-end XAA flow. To learn more, read [Test Cross App Access (XAA) Flow](/docs/secure/call-apis-on-users-behalf/xaa/end-to-end-testing).
