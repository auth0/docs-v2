This page walks through configuring a generic SAML identity provider for Cross App Access (XAA). Users authenticate into the enterprise IdP via SAML, but the IdP must also expose an OIDC issuer or discovery endpoint — Auth0 uses this to validate the signature of incoming ID-JAGs. You'll register the Requesting App and Resource App with your upstream IdP, configure a Cross App Access policy in the IdP, and configure a SAML connection so Auth0 can federate with your IdP.

## Prerequisites

Before getting started, you need a SAML enterprise IdP that also exposes an OIDC issuer or discovery endpoint, and supports issuing an [Identity Assertion Authorization Grant](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/draft-ietf-oauth-identity-assertion-authz-grant/) (ID-JAG). Confirm with your IdP vendor that Cross App Access is supported and enabled for your tenant.

## Register the Requesting App in your IdP

Register the Requesting App as a SAML application in your upstream IdP. This is the application your users authenticate as when signing in to the Requesting App.

Note the Requesting App's identifier in your IdP; you'll need it later when configuring the Cross App Access policy.

## Register the Resource App in your IdP

Register the Resource App as a SAML application in your upstream IdP. This application represents your Auth0 tenant to the IdP.

Note the Resource App's **Sign In URL** (the SAML SSO URL for this application) in your IdP; you'll need it when creating the Auth0 connection below.

## Configure the Cross App Access policy

In your IdP, configure the Cross App Access authorization policy to allow the Requesting App to connect to the Resource App. Refer to your IdP's documentation for how to define this policy — the specifics vary by vendor, but the policy must associate the Requesting App and the Resource App so that the IdP will issue an ID-JAG between them.

## Configure a SAML connection in Auth0

In your Resource App's Auth0 tenant, [create a SAML connection](/docs/authenticate/identity-providers/enterprise-identity-providers/configure-saml-identity-provider) pointing to your IdP, using the **Sign In URL** of the Resource App you registered in your IdP.

To configure the connection for XAA:

1. Under the connection's OIDC settings, enter your upstream IdP's OIDC issuer or discovery URL. Auth0 uses this to fetch the signing keys it needs to validate incoming ID-JAGs.
2. Enable the **Cross App Access - Resource Application** role using the Auth0 Dashboard or Management API:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Auth0 Dashboard">
    Toggle on **Cross App Access - Resource Application**. This enables your Resource App to accept ID-JAGs issued by the enterprise IdP associated with this connection.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Management API">
    Make a `PATCH` request to the [`/connections/{YOUR_CONNECTION_ID}`](/docs/api/management/v2/connections/patch-connections-by-id) endpoint and set `cross_app_access_resource_app` to `active: true`:

    ```bash
    curl -L -X PATCH 'https://<YOUR_DOMAIN>/api/v2/connections/<YOUR_CONNECTION_ID>' \
      -H 'Content-Type: application/json' \
      -H 'Authorization: Bearer <YOUR_MANAGEMENT_API_TOKEN>' \
      -d '{"cross_app_access_resource_app": {"active": true}}'
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Callout icon="triangle-exclamation" color="#F59E0B" iconType="regular">

Cross App Access is incompatible with SAML connections that use a custom user identifier. Configuration will fail if XAA is enabled and a custom user identifier is defined on the connection.

</Callout>

Once the connection is created, you are ready to test the end-to-end XAA flow. To learn more, read [Test Cross App Access (XAA) Flow](/docs/secure/call-apis-on-users-behalf/xaa/end-to-end-testing).
