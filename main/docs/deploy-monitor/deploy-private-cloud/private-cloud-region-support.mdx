Auth0 Private Cloud can be deployed in multiple cloud regions worldwide on both AWS and Azure. This page explains region availability, the validation process, and how to request deployment in additional regions.

## Production-Ready Regions

Production-ready regions have completed Auth0's rigorous validation process and are immediately available for Private Cloud deployment. These regions meet Auth0's standards for high availability, performance, security, and operational support.

### What Makes a Region Production-Ready?

All production-ready regions undergo comprehensive validation:

<Accordion title="Multi-Availability Zone Deployment">
  Auth0 validates that the region supports deployment across multiple availability zones (AZs) to ensure high availability.

  - **AWS:** Minimum 3 availability zones
  - **Azure:** Minimum 3 availability zones with proper architectural support
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="High Availability (GeoHA) Pairing">
  For customers requiring geographic redundancy, Auth0 validates GeoHA pairing between two regions.

  - Validates network latency between paired regions
  - Confirms data replication capabilities
  - Tests failover and fallback procedures
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Capacity and Performance Testing">
  Auth0 validates that the region has sufficient capacity for Private Cloud workloads and meets performance benchmarks.

  - Cloud provider quota verification
  - Load testing at subscribed RPS levels
  - Network throughput validation
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Security and Compliance">
  Auth0 validates security controls and compliance requirements.

  - Encryption at rest and in transit
  - Security monitoring and alerting
  - Compliance certifications (where applicable)
</Accordion>

## Region Validation Process

If your data residency requirements include a region not listed as production-ready, Auth0 can validate additional regions as part of your Private Cloud engagement.

### Validation Timeline

| Cloud Provider | Typical Timeline | Complexity Factors |
|----------------|------------------|-------------------|
| **AWS** | 1-4 weeks | Straightforward in most regions. Can extend if GeoHA pairing requires validation of second region. |
| **Azure** | 2-8 weeks | More complex due to availability zone architecture, capacity constraints, and GeoHA pairing challenges. Microsoft coordination may be required. |

<Warning>
  **Important:** Region validation timelines are estimates. Some Azure regions may not be feasible for Private Cloud deployment due to infrastructure limitations, capacity constraints, or unavailable GeoHA pairing options.
</Warning>

### What Happens During Validation?

The validation process ensures production-grade reliability before deployment:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Feasibility Assessment" titleSize="h3" stepNumber={1}>
    Auth0 assesses whether the requested region meets Private Cloud requirements.

    - Availability zone support
    - GeoHA pairing availability (if required)
    - Cloud provider capacity and quotas
    - Network connectivity and latency
  </Step>

  <Step title="Infrastructure Provisioning" titleSize="h3" stepNumber={2}>
    Auth0 provisions test infrastructure in the target region.

    - Multi-AZ deployment
    - Network configuration
    - Security controls
    - Monitoring and alerting
  </Step>

  <Step title="Validation Testing" titleSize="h3" stepNumber={3}>
    Auth0 runs comprehensive tests to validate the deployment.

    - Functional testing (authentication flows, APIs, etc.)
    - Performance testing (RPS capacity, latency)
    - High availability testing (AZ failover)
    - GeoHA testing (if applicable)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Production Approval" titleSize="h3" stepNumber={4}>
    Once validation is complete, the region is approved for production use.

    - Customer environment provisioning begins
    - Region added to production-ready list (for future customers)
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Known Challenges

#### Azure Region Complexity

Azure regions require more extensive validation due to architectural complexity:

- **Availability Zone Support:** Not all Azure regions support the 3-AZ architecture required for Private Cloud
- **Capacity Constraints:** Azure may have capacity limitations in certain regions, requiring Microsoft coordination to allocate resources
- **GeoHA Pairing Issues:** Some Azure region pairs have incompatible architectures or unsupported configurations

**Example:** Azure South Africa validation encountered architectural challenges that made GeoHA deployment infeasible, requiring the customer to pivot to Azure Europe.

#### GeoHA Pairing Validation

If your deployment requires GeoHA, Auth0 must validate **both** regions and the pairing between them:

- Doubles validation timeline (both regions must be validated)
- Requires compatible architectures in both regions
- Network latency between regions must meet requirements

### Cost Considerations

**No additional cost for standard region validation.** Region validation is included as part of your Private Cloud engagement when:
- You have an active Private Cloud contract or purchase order
- Validation occurs during your onboarding window

**Infrastructure changes may incur costs.** If validation reveals infrastructure limitations requiring custom solutions, Auth0 will provide a cost estimate before proceeding.

## Requesting Region Validation

To request validation for a region not listed as production-ready:

### 1. Contact Auth0 During Sales Cycle

<Callout icon="file-lines" color="#0EA5E9" iconType="regular">
  **Best Practice:** Discuss region requirements with your Auth0 Account Executive **before** contract signing to ensure validation can complete before your deployment timeline.
</Callout>

Provide the following information:
- **Primary region:** Your preferred deployment region
- **Secondary region:** (If GeoHA required) Your preferred failover region
- **Timeline:** When you need the environment ready for production
- **Business context:** Data residency requirements, compliance needs, proximity to users

### 2. Auth0 Feasibility Assessment

Auth0 will assess feasibility within 5 business days:
- Region architecture review
- Capacity and quota check with cloud provider
- GeoHA pairing validation (if applicable)
- Estimated validation timeline

**Possible outcomes:**
- ✅ **Feasible:** Validation can proceed with estimated timeline
- ⚠️ **Feasible with caveats:** Validation possible but may encounter challenges
- ❌ **Not feasible:** Region cannot support Private Cloud requirements (Auth0 will recommend alternatives)

### 3. Validation Execution

If feasible, Auth0 begins validation:
- Validation can run in parallel with contract finalization
- Auth0 provides weekly status updates
- Any blockers are escalated immediately

### 4. Production Deployment

After successful validation:
- Your Private Cloud environment is provisioned in the validated region
- Onboarding proceeds according to standard timeline
- Region is added to production-ready list for future customers

## Frequently Asked Questions

<Accordion title="Can I deploy in a region not listed on the docs?">
  Yes, but the region must be validated first. Contact your Auth0 Account Executive early in your sales cycle to request validation.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How long does region validation take?">
  - **AWS:** Typically 1-4 weeks
  - **Azure:** Typically 2-8 weeks

  GeoHA deployments require validation of both regions, which extends the timeline.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if my preferred region cannot be validated?">
  Auth0 will work with you to identify alternative regions that meet your data residency requirements. In most cases, nearby regions in the same geography are viable alternatives.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Does region validation cost extra?">
  No, standard region validation is included with your Private Cloud engagement. Custom infrastructure requirements may incur additional costs, which Auth0 will discuss upfront.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I validate a region after contract signing?">
  Yes, but it may delay your production deployment timeline. Auth0 strongly recommends requesting validation during your sales cycle to ensure the region is ready when you need it.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Why is Azure validation more complex than AWS?">
  Azure regions have more variability in architecture, capacity, and availability zone support. Some Azure regions also have GeoHA pairing challenges that require extensive coordination with Microsoft.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens if validation uncovers issues?">
  Auth0 will work with the cloud provider to resolve issues when possible. If issues cannot be resolved, Auth0 will recommend alternative regions and restart validation if needed.
</Accordion>
