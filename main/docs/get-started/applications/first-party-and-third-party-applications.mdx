---
title: First-Party and Third-Party Applications
description: "Understand the difference between first-party and third-party applications in Auth0, and when to use each type."
---

When you register an application in Auth0, you decide whether it is first-party or third-party based on who owns and operates it. This choice determines the security controls, consent requirements, and available features for the application.

- **First-party applications** are owned and operated by your organization. You control their deployment, credentials, and behavior. You are accountable for what they do.
- **Third-party applications** are owned and operated by an external organization — a partner, an independent developer, or an AI agent. You grant them access to your resources, but you cannot directly control what they do with that access.

<Callout icon="file-lines" color="#0EA5E9" iconType="regular">
"Third-party" refers to operational control, not authorship. Many organizations outsource development of their own applications — a contractor building your application does not make it third-party. The key distinction is: who deploys it, who holds the credentials, and who can stop it.
</Callout>

## How they differ

| | **First-party** | **Third-party** |
|---|---|---|
| **API access** | Follows the API's configured access policy | Always requires an explicit [client grant](/docs/get-started/applications/application-access-to-apis-client-grants) |
| **User consent** | Can be skipped (if enabled on the API) | Always required |
| **Grant types** | All supported grant types | `authorization_code` and `refresh_token` |
| **OIDC** | Supported | Not supported (this release) |
| **Client ID format** | Standard format | `tpc_` prefix |
| **Connections** | All enabled connections | Domain-level connections |

For detailed capabilities and restrictions of third-party applications, read [Third-Party Applications](/docs/get-started/applications/third-party-applications).

## Two ways to classify applications

Auth0 uses two independent classifications for applications:

- **Confidential or public** describes the application's authentication capability — whether it can securely hold a client secret. To learn more, read [Confidential and Public Applications](/docs/get-started/applications/confidential-and-public-applications).
- **First-party or third-party** describes the trust relationship — who owns and operates the application.

These are orthogonal. Both first-party and third-party applications can be confidential (Regular Web App) or public (SPA, Native). A third-party Regular Web App is both confidential and third-party.

## Check application ownership

To check if an application is first-party or third-party:

**Via the Dashboard:**

1. Navigate to **Auth0 Dashboard > Applications > Applications**.
2. Select the application.
3. Third-party applications display a badge indicating they are third-party.

`[SCREENSHOT PLACEHOLDER: Dashboard — application settings showing third-party badge. REVIEW: confirm badge design and placement]`

**Via the Management API:**

Check the `is_first_party` property in the application response. Third-party applications have `is_first_party: false`.

`[CODE PLACEHOLDER: curl GET /api/v2/clients/CLIENT_ID showing is_first_party and third_party_security_mode]`

## Set application ownership

You choose first-party or third-party ownership when creating the application. For third-party applications, set `is_first_party` to `false`:

`[CODE PLACEHOLDER: curl POST /api/v2/clients with is_first_party: false]`

<Callout icon="file-lines" color="#0EA5E9" iconType="regular">
Applications created through [Dynamic Client Registration](/docs/get-started/applications/dynamic-client-registration) are always third-party.
</Callout>
